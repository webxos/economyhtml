Shahada Study Guide in Sunni Islam

The Shahada is the Islamic declaration of faith, the first pillar of Islam in Sunni tradition. It affirms belief in the oneness of God (Allah) and the prophethood of Muhammad. Below is the Shahada in Arabic transliteration, its English translation, and a breakdown of each word with definitions.

The Shahada

Arabic Transliteration: Ashhadu an la ilaha illa Allah, wa ashhadu anna Muhammadan rasul Allah

English Translation: I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah

Summary

The Shahada consists of two parts:

1. La ilaha illa Allah: Declares the monotheistic belief that there is no deity worthy of worship except Allah.

2. Muhammadan rasul Allah: Affirms that Muhammad is the final Messenger sent by Allah.

Reciting the Shahada with sincerity and understanding is the formal act of entering Islam and is a fundamental expression of faith for Muslims.
